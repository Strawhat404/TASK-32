Create a VaultRoom Commerce & Experience platform that lets a multi-location escape-room and game shop run fully offline while managing merchandise, bookings, and community engagement in one interface. The Svelte web UI provides role-based navigation for Administrator, Store Manager, Inventory Clerk, Moderator, and Customer/Member users, with clear inline validation and undo prompts for risky actions. Staff can maintain master data for SKUs, barcodes, batch/lot attributes, packaging specs, bin locations, carriers, and customers, with a “coding rules” screen to define human-readable identifiers (for example, “SKU-YYYYMM-####”) that have effective dates and automatically expire on a chosen MM/DD/YYYY at 11:59 PM. The storefront experience includes a cart and checkout flow that supports cross-store and same-store cart merge (latest edit wins per line item, quantities sum with a cap), bundle-threshold prompts such as “Add $10.00 more for 10% off,” and promotion stacking rules where at most one coupon may be combined with member pricing but never with threshold discounts; purchase limits like “max 2 per customer per day” must display before checkout. Customers can choose pickup, local delivery, or shipment using locally configured rate tables, with sales tax calculated from stored jurisdiction rules; final placement performs real-time price and inventory checks and blocks checkout if any item changed by more than 2% or stock is insufficient. For the experience side, staff manage script listings (tags, difficulty 1–5, duration in minutes, party-size range, required props inventory, and active/paused status) and store resources (rooms/tables, business hours, host schedules, and availability). A built-in forum supports sections/subsections, threads with replies and nested replies, topic tags, and moderation states including pinned, featured, locked, and archived, plus a scoring page that generates session results using weighted aggregation, selectable missing-value strategy (drop, zero-fill, or average-fill), mapping rules, multi-round merging, and an auditable review adjustment history; optional grades and rankings can be shown per store and date range.

The backend uses Fastify to expose offline-first REST-style APIs consumed by the Svelte frontend, enforcing server-side validation, concurrency controls, and consistent pricing/inventory snapshots before order creation. PostgreSQL is the single local system of record for master data, forum content, carts/orders, scripts/resources, schedules, and scoring artifacts, with import/export via local CSV files limited to 20 MB and deduplication rules that merge customers by exact email match or normalized phone match while preserving an immutable change log. Security and privacy compliance are implemented without any external services: authentication is local username/password only, passwords are salted and hashed with Argon2id, accounts lock for 15 minutes after 5 failed attempts, and admin sessions idle-timeout after 20 minutes. All traffic is served over HTTPS/TLS on the local network using a locally installed certificate; sensitive fields (such as customer addresses and notes) are encrypted at rest with AES-256, and masked by default in the UI (for example, only last 4 digits of phone). Data minimization and consent are captured with explicit checkboxes for marketing preferences, and the system supports on-demand data export, deletion requests with a 7-day reversible window for administrators, and retention rules such as purging archived forum logs after 365 days while preserving aggregated, non-identifying score summaries for reporting.
